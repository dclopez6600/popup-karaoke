The Story Behind PopUp Karaoke

PopUp Karaoke started in 2020, in the middle of a pandemic, during a time when people were desperate for any reason to come together. I'm Daniel — a U.S. Army veteran from NW Indiana — and what began as a way to bring some life back to my community turned into something I couldn't have predicted: a full-time mobile karaoke operation that has run hundreds of events across Northwest Indiana and the greater Chicago area.

Before PopUp Karaoke, my background was service. The military taught me things that I carry into every event: show up prepared, don't cut corners, be the most reliable person in the room. When I started doing karaoke shows, I brought the same standard to the mic stand. You get there early. You set up right. You don't leave until the client is happy. That's not a business model — it's just how I was raised and trained.

Why Karaoke?

It sounds like a simple question, but the honest answer is that karaoke is one of the only entertainment formats that makes strangers into friends. Think about what actually happens at a karaoke night: someone who was standing at the back of the room, arms crossed, insisting they weren't going to sing — ends up at the front of the room at 11pm, getting a standing ovation. That transformation is real. It happens at almost every single event we run.

Music is the fastest path to a feeling. When someone hears the opening notes of a song they grew up with, they're not standing in a banquet hall anymore — they're back in the car with their best friend at 17, or at a school dance, or at a concert they saved up for. Karaoke takes that private feeling and makes it communal. That's what I got addicted to watching happen, and it's why I built a business around it.

What Makes Us Different

We're hosts, not just equipment operators

The difference between a karaoke machine and a karaoke night is the person running it. We read the room, manage energy, encourage first-timers, and keep the queue moving in a way that keeps everyone engaged — not just the confident singers.

🔊

Professional-grade equipment, every time

Concert-quality PA speakers, two wireless microphones, LED stage lighting, and 61,000+ songs. No consumer gear, no Bluetooth speakers, no tablet apps. Equipment that makes singers sound good and makes the room feel like an event.

Veteran standards, every event

On time, fully prepared, and professional from first contact to final song. Military service taught me that reliability isn't optional — it's the baseline. We don't show up late, we don't cut setups short, and we don't leave messes behind.

📍

Local — actually local

We're based in NW Indiana. Not a franchise that subcontracts out. Not a Chicago company charging travel fees to come to Merrillville. We know the venues, we know the communities, and we care about this area because we live here.
